01 — Foundation

The Book Cover as the Blueprint

The DLAB brand system began with what already worked: the book cover’s visual language and the logo embedded on it. Instead of reinventing the brand, I treated the cover as the source of truth — extracting cues for icon shapes, color behavior, type hierarchy, and the pattern style that later became central across applications.

What was extracted

  • Logo anchor: The heart-in-cart mark (client-provided) was reinforced as the primary brand cue — it appears on the book and became the recognizable anchor for the system.
  • Palette behavior: The book informed the core color language — confident blue + signal red, supported by calm light tones.
  • Language: The messaging tone and phrasing style on the book guided how the brand speaks across layouts and applications.
  • Built beyond the book: The icon system, heart / heart-in-cart wallpaper, and final font selections were created to strengthen the brand and expand it into a full identity system.

Outcome: a system that feels “born from the book,” not layered on top of it.

07 — Pattern

Heart & Heart-in-Cart Wallpaper System

The heart wallpaper became the connective tissue across DLAB applications. Built from the heart and heart-in-cart forms, it works like a brand “texture” — adding recognition without competing with typography or message. This pattern was used repeatedly across layouts to unify everything from socials to large-format installs.

Primary Wallpaper

The main repeat pattern used as a background system — designed to scale from subtle texture to bold brand presence.

Variant A

Pattern Usage Notes

  • Center of applications: Used as a consistent backdrop across print, social, and event assets.
  • Hierarchy-first: Pattern supports the content; it never competes with headline legibility.
  • Scales cleanly: Works as subtle texture or bold brand statement depending on density.
  • Color discipline: Blue leads; red is used as selective emphasis.
